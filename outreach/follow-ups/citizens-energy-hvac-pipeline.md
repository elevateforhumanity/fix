I wanted to briefly outline our structured HVAC pathway, specifically how the On-the-Job Training (OJT) internship component integrates with employer partnerships.

Our model is built as a workforce-aligned pathway that progresses candidates through three stages:

**1. Foundational Instruction**
Participants complete structured HVAC fundamentals, safety training, and EPA Section 608 certification preparation through our career and technical institute. This ensures candidates enter the field with baseline technical knowledge, safety awareness, and credential readiness.

**2. OJT Internship Placement (Employer-Aligned)**
After completing the instructional phase, participants transition into a supervised On-the-Job Training (OJT) internship with an employer partner. This allows them to gain real-world, hands-on experience in a controlled workforce setting while continuing competency development.

**3. Employment Pipeline**
Following successful OJT participation, candidates transition into long-term employment aligned with industry demand.

In addition, our programs are aligned with workforce funding frameworks including WIOA and Workforce Ready Grant (when applicable). These funding pathways can support training reimbursement and workforce development investments, helping reduce the financial burden on both trainees and employer partners while strengthening the talent pipeline.
