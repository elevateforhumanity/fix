# CRS Training Provider Approval + Fee Waiver Request

**From:** Elizabeth Lene Greene, Executive Director, Selfish Inc.
**In partnership with:** Elevate for Humanity
**Date:** June 2026

---

## Version 1 — To INARR

**To:** info@inarr.org
**Subject:** CRS Training Provider Approval + Fee Waiver Request (Elevate for Humanity / Selfish Inc.)

Dear INARR Team,

My name is Elizabeth Lene Greene, and I serve as Executive Director of Selfish Inc., a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization based in Indianapolis.

Elevate for Humanity, in partnership with Selfish Inc., is working to establish a Certified Recovery Specialist (CRS) training program through the Elevate for Humanity Career & Training Institute. This initiative is designed to expand access to credentialed recovery support services for underserved populations, including individuals impacted by substance use and justice system involvement.

The CRS program is currently approved on Indiana's Eligible Training Provider List (ETPL) through WorkOne, which means eligible participants can access WIOA funding to cover training costs at no out-of-pocket expense. The program is also structured to align with Workforce Ready Grant (WRG) and related public workforce funding streams.

The program is delivered in a hybrid format. Didactic instruction is completed online through the Elevate for Humanity platform. Hands-on field training is completed at employer partner sites.

We are writing to respectfully request a waiver of the CRS training provider application fee. Granting this waiver will allow us to accelerate program launch and immediately begin serving individuals who face financial and systemic barriers to accessing credentialed training. We are prepared to launch our first cohort within 60–90 days of approval.

We are fully committed to meeting all CRS training standards, including curriculum alignment, supervision requirements, and reporting obligations, and are prepared to provide any supporting documentation needed.

Thank you for your consideration. We welcome the opportunity to work in alignment with Indiana's recovery and workforce priorities.

Sincerely,
Elizabeth Lene Greene
Executive Director, Selfish Inc.
In partnership with Elevate for Humanity
