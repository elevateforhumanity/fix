# CRS Training Program — Supervision Structure

**Program:** Certified Recovery Specialist (CRS)
**Provider:** Elevate for Humanity Career & Training Institute
**Partner Organization:** Selfish Inc. (501(c)(3))
**Credential Authority:** Indiana DMHA / INARR
**Document Purpose:** Submitted in support of CRS training provider approval and fee waiver request

---

## Overview

Indiana DMHA requires that CRS training programs maintain a defined supervision structure throughout training delivery. This document describes the supervision chain, contact ratios, documentation requirements, and escalation procedures for the Elevate for Humanity CRS program.

---

## Supervision Chain

```
Program Director / Administrator (Elizabeth Lene Greene, Selfish Inc.)
  └── Lead Instructor (credentialed CRS or CPRS)
        ├── Module Instructors (credentialed CRS or CPRS)
        └── Learner Cohort
              └── Practicum Supervisor (Module 8 field hours)
```

### Roles and Responsibilities

**Program Director / Administrator — Elizabeth Lene Greene**
- Overall accountability for curriculum alignment with DMHA/INARR standards
- Recruits, vets, and approves all instructors before learner contact
- Reviews and signs off on learner completion records
- Point of contact for DMHA/INARR compliance inquiries
- Receives and acts on all escalated concerns within 24 hours
- Does not deliver direct curriculum instruction

**Lead Instructor (credentialed CRS or CPRS)**
- Delivers core curriculum content (Modules 1–7)
- Conducts weekly check-ins with enrolled learners
- Monitors attendance and engagement; flags concerns to Program Director
- Completes module completion sign-offs in the LMS (Elevate for Humanity platform)
- Holds active CRS or CPRS credential in good standing with DMHA

**Module Instructors (Guest SMEs)**
- Deliver assigned module content under Lead Instructor oversight
- Report attendance and participation to Lead Instructor after each session
- Do not make independent decisions about learner advancement or dismissal
- Supervised by Lead Instructor; escalate concerns upward

**Practicum Supervisor (Module 8)**
- Supervises learner field hours (minimum 8 hours for CRS practicum component)
- Holds active CRS, CPRS, LCSW, or equivalent credential
- Completes practicum sign-off form for each learner
- Reports any safety or conduct concerns to Program Director immediately

---

## Learner-to-Supervisor Ratios

| Setting | Maximum Ratio |
|---------|--------------|
| Classroom / online instruction | 20 learners : 1 instructor |
| Small group practice sessions | 8 learners : 1 instructor |
| Practicum field supervision | 5 learners : 1 practicum supervisor |
| Individual check-in sessions | 1:1 |

Cohort size for the initial launch is capped at **15 learners** to ensure adequate supervision during the first delivery cycle.

---

## Supervision Schedule

### During Training (Modules 1–7)

| Frequency | Activity |
|-----------|----------|
| Weekly | Lead Instructor check-in with full cohort (30 min, group) |
| Bi-weekly | Individual learner progress check (15 min, 1:1 or small group) |
| Per module | Module completion review before advancing to next module |
| As needed | Escalation meeting with Program Director |

### During Practicum (Module 8)

| Frequency | Activity |
|-----------|----------|
| Per session | Practicum supervisor present or reachable by phone |
| Weekly | Practicum supervisor submits hours log to Lead Instructor |
| End of practicum | Practicum supervisor completes sign-off form |

## Documentation Requirements

The following records are maintained in the Elevate for Humanity LMS and/or paper files:

| Record | Maintained By | Retention |
|--------|--------------|-----------|
| Attendance log (per session) | Lead Instructor | 3 years |
| Module completion sign-offs | Lead Instructor | 3 years |
| Learner progress notes | Lead Instructor | 3 years |
| Practicum hours log | Practicum Supervisor | 3 years |
| Practicum sign-off form | Practicum Supervisor | 3 years |
| Incident/concern reports | Program Director | 5 years |
| Completion certificates | Program Director | 5 years |

All records are available for DMHA/INARR review upon request within 5 business days.

## Escalation Procedures

### Learner Conduct or Safety Concern

1. Instructor identifies concern during session
2. Instructor documents concern in writing (date, description, action taken)
3. Instructor notifies Lead Instructor within 24 hours
4. Lead Instructor notifies Program Director within 24 hours
5. Program Director determines response: additional support, corrective action, or dismissal
6. If immediate safety risk: contact 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) or 911 as appropriate; notify Program Director immediately

### Instructor Conduct Concern

1. Concern reported to Program Director directly (bypass Lead Instructor if Lead Instructor is the subject)
2. Program Director investigates within 48 hours
3. Instructor suspended from learner contact pending investigation
4. Resolution documented and retained on file

### Credential Lapse

1. Instructor notifies Program Director immediately upon credential lapse or suspension
2. Instructor suspended from instruction until credential is reinstated
3. Program Director arranges coverage for affected sessions

## Mandatory Reporting

All instructors and supervisors are mandatory reporters under Indiana law. Any suspected abuse, neglect, or exploitation of a vulnerable adult must be reported to:

- **Indiana Adult Protective Services:** 1-800-992-6978
- **Indiana Child Abuse Hotline:** 1-800-800-5556 (if minors are involved)

Reports must be made immediately and documented. Program Director must be notified of any mandatory report within 24 hours.

---

## Supervision During Remote/Online Delivery

When training is delivered via the Elevate for Humanity online platform:

- Lead Instructor monitors session participation in real time
- Learners must have camera on during live sessions (accommodations available upon request)
- Chat and discussion board activity is reviewed by Lead Instructor within 24 hours
- Technical issues that prevent participation are documented and accommodated within 48 hours
